33. Use a TWO (02) month moving average to generate a forecast for demand in month 6. Demand for months 1 to 5 are:

Month 1 2 3 4 5
Demand (in 10,000 units) 23 37 29 33 27

Explanation: The two-month moving averages are calculated as follows:

  • Month 2 = (23 + 37) / 2 = 30
  • Month 3 = (37 + 29) / 2 = 33
  • Month 4 = (29 + 33) / 2 = 31
  • Month 5 = (33 + 27) / 2 = 30

Therefore, the forecast for month 6 is the moving average from month 5 = 30 * 10,000 = 300,000.

34. The demand for a product (in 10,000 units) in each of the last FIVE (05) months is shown below:

Month 1 2 3 4 5
Demand 33 27 39 23 37

Apply Exponential Smoothing with a smoothing constant of 0.5 to generate a forecast for demand in month 6.

Solution: Applying exponential smoothing with a smoothing constant of 0.5 we get:

  • F1 = Y1 = 33.00 (assumed)
  • F2 = 0.5 × 27.00 + 0.5 × 33.00 = 30.00
  • F3 = 0.5 × 39.00 + 0.5 × 30.00 = 34.50
  • F4 = 0.5 × 23.00 + 0.5 × 34.50 = 28.75
  • F5 = 0.5 × 37.00 + 0.5 × 28.75 = 32.88

Forecast for month 6 = 32.88 × 10,000 = 328,800
